Komodo Collaboration Komodo IDE only

Collaboration

With Komodo Collaboration you can collaboratively edit documents inside Komodo with several people at the same time. All changes are transmitted in near real-time, just as you know it from other tools like Google Docs. All content that you create with Komodo Collaboration is stored on the ActiveState Collaboration server until you delete it. Note that the Collaboration content is not encrypted. Only your authorized Contacts will be able to see and edit it.

Account Setup

Before you can start using Collaboration, you have to set up your ActiveState account in Komodo. If you do not have one, you can create one for free at: https://account.activestate.com/signup/

To set up the account, select Tools|Komodo Services|My Account from the menu. Enter the email address and password of your ActiveState account and click Login. If your login data was valid, you should see the message "You're logged in to Komodo Services".

Collaboration Setup

You can now open the Collaboration tab in the left sidebar and click the Enable Komodo Collaboration button.

Using Collaboration

When Collaboration is enabled and connected to the server, the Collaboration tab on the left sidebar displays two different lists: Sessions and Contacts.

A Collaboration Session is a collection of hosted documents that you can collaboratively edit with other people. Click the plus icon in the top right corner of the Collaboration tab to create your first session. After you have given the session a name, it should appear in your Sessions list.

Initially, your session will not contain any documents, so to start collaborating, just add a document by right-clicking on the session name and selecting Add > Empty Document from the context menu. After you have given the document a name it appears under its session in the Sessions list. Double-click on the document to open it. You can also share an existing file by opening it in Komodo and then right-clicking on the document tab and selecting Collaboration->Share.

Contacts are other users that you can add to a Session to give them access to all documents within that session. Add your first contact by clicking the plus icon in the top right corner of the Contacts panel and entering the email address of the person you would like to collaborate with. Note that the email address has to be registered with the ActiveState.com account server. The contact will not appear in your list instantly. Instead, the person you have added will receive a notification in the bottom right corner of their Komodo window that asks them to confirm you as their Collaboration Contact. Once they have confirmed you as their Contact they will appear in your Contacts list, and you can add them to your session by right clicking on the session name and selecting Add > User to this Session > (Their name). Your Session will then appear in their Sessions list and you can start editing documents together at the same time.

Collaborative Editing

Editing text in a Komodo Collaboration tab generally works the same as with any other document. However, there are a couple of differences that you should know.

Each tab that belongs to a Collaboration document has a small green circle icon in its tab header. This icon indicates that a connection to the Collaboration server has been established, and it turns red if an error occurs (if you lose the connection to the server).

You will also notice some colored highlights in Collaboration documents that you won't see in normal Komodo documents: All the text you write will be highlighted with a light-blue background. This distinguishes your changes from the original text as well as being distinguishable from the modifications made by other users, which will have a light-green background. Green and red margins on the side help you keep track of the lines that have had text modifications - a green margin indicates text was added, whilst a red margin indicates text was removed.

Additionally, the behaviour of the undo and redo buttons is slightly different than you might expect it: You can never undo any changes that have been made by other users. That means your undo history is discarded whenever another user makes a change to the document.
